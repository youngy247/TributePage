Steven Gerrard

The longest Liverpool FC captain ever

The One Club Captain

Steven 'George' Gerrard is a retired professional footballer who is best known for as being a Liverpool FC legend. Having spent the majority of his playing career at the club from 1998 to 2015, for many years he was the lifeblood of his childhood club. He made 504 Premier League appearances for the Reds across 12 seasons (2003-2015) as captain, scoring over 180 goals and winning numerous trophies, including the infamous Champions League in Istanbul, FA Cup, and the UEFA Cup. For the 2004-05 season, Gerrard was presented with the number 8 shirt- the shirt number he worn ever since.

Being the well rounded box to box midfielder that he was enabled a call up for England, making his international debut against Ukraine on 31 May 2000. His versatility and wide range of skills meant he was capable of playing in many positions. This was acknowledge by onlookers thus granting him the captains armband and went on to captain england 38 times and got 114 caps for his country.
